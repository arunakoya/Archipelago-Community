Archipelago Anektos - Climate Initiative

The Archipelago is a collaborative network of organisations focused on shared innovation, governance, and resource optimisation. Member organisations work together on cross-industry projects, exchanging knowledge and creating standards that benefit all participants.

The Climate Initiative goal is simple.
Slow down the global temperature increase, by reducing the CO2 emissions, providing alternative energy sources. Our governance structure ensures that all members have a voice, while a neutral operator facilitates coordination and compliance across all initiatives. Each project is tracked through metrics dashboards to measure efficiency, engagement, and outcomes.

Participation in the initiative allows organisations to access shared services, reduce duplication, and participate in joint ventures with reduced risk. Members benefit from collaboration, transparency, and access to insights that would be challenging to develop independently.

Climate Initiative started off with four countries, US, Russia, China and India. The goal was clear, and the quickest win was to reduce the use of the fossil fuel powered factories, profoundly.

China have made significant progress, but are only in Year One of a five year strategy. One approach is to ramp down dependence on oil, and ramp up renewables and other more sustainable energy options.

Six months, from when the Climate Initiative was started, there is sight of the Paris Agreement being met. This is a major winning outcome. Studies of people displaced due to climate change is showing that it is mainly due to the temperature rise. So meeting the Paris Agreement, will prevent further climate change displacement in other countries.

Title: Archipelago Climate Action Advisory Initiative: Reducing CO₂ Emissions to Limit Temperature Rise and Mitigate Climate Displacement Prepared by: Aruna Koya Date: February 15, 2026 --- 1. Purpose & Rationale Objective:To establish a neutral, cross-border advisory initiative that provides rigorous, data-driven analysis of the global and national impacts of CO₂ emissions reductions, with a focus on mitigating climate-related displacement and population risk. Rationale: - Rising CO₂ levels are directly linked to global temperature increases, which drive extreme weather, sea-level rise, and displacement of vulnerable populations. - Governments and multilateral organizations require neutral, quantitative analysis to prioritize mitigation strategies and investments. - Existing climate initiatives often focus on advocacy or technical mitigation measures without connecting directly to sovereign economic and societal risk from displacement. Impact: - Provide actionable insights for policy-makers, international organizations, and finance institutions. - Strengthen the economic, social, and humanitarian case for accelerated emission reductions. - Enable proactive planning to reduce displacement and population vulnerability. --- 2. Structure & Governance Entity Type: - Independent, non-profit advisory initiative registered under United Kingdom. Governance: - Advisory Board: 7–10 members with geographic and sector diversity, including: - Former climate negotiators or ministers - Climate scientists and macro-economists - Disaster risk and displacement specialists - Multilateral development and humanitarian experts - Executive Coordinator: Responsible for research, convening, and dissemination - Conflict-of-interest policies: Strict neutrality, non-partisan, non-advocacy Decision-Making: - Consensus-driven approval of research methodology and outputs - Transparent, evidence-based methodology for all analysis --- 3. Initiative Scope & Outputs Time Horizon: 2026–2035 Key Outputs: Analytical Briefings – Quantitative papers assessing national and global CO₂ reduction pathways and projected temperature trajectories. Climate Displacement Risk Index – A tool quantifying potential population displacement under different emission scenarios. Private Roundtables – Closed-door convenings of governments, finance institutions, and multilateral agencies. Annual Strategic Report – Data-driven assessment of mitigation progress, temperature projections, and population displacement risk. --- 4. Methodology - Modelling global CO₂ trajectories against temperature rise scenarios (IPCC-aligned). - Quantifying displacement risk by country and region using demographic and climate data. - Economic assessment of mitigation vs. adaptation costs. - Scenario planning to inform policy priorities and investment. All methodology will be peer-reviewed by advisory board members and external experts. --- 5. Strategic Alignment Outputs are designed to inform: - G20 finance and climate tracks - Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development economic and environmental outlooks - UNFCCC adaptation and mitigation dialogues Neutral framing ensures credibility across countries, political contexts, and sectors. --- 6. Timeline (First 12 Months) - Month 1: Finalize charter, governance, and mandate - Month 2: Recruit founding advisory board members - Month 3: Produce first analytical briefing on CO₂ reduction impact and displacement risk - Months 4–12: Develop Climate Displacement Risk Index, host private advisory roundtable, publish annual report --- 7. Resource Requirements - Funding for research team, modelling software, and convening logistics - Secretariat support for coordination and data management - Advisory board stipends and travel (if applicable) - Confidential dissemination of reports and briefings --- 8. Next Steps Circulate concept note to potential founding advisors for feedback Establish legal and governance structure Begin recruitment of core research and modelling team Schedule first advisory board roundtable --- Prepared by:Aruna Koya, Archipelago Climate Initiative - Reducing CO₂ Emissions to Limit Temperature Rise and Mitigate Climate Displacement
